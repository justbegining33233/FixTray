# FixTray Shop Participation Agreement

Effective Date: [DATE]

This Shop Participation Agreement (Agreement) is entered into by and between:

1. FixTray, Inc., a [STATE] corporation with a principal place of business at [ADDRESS] (FixTray), and
2. [LEGAL SHOP NAME], a [ENTITY TYPE] organized under the laws of [STATE/COUNTRY], with a principal place of business at [ADDRESS] (Shop).

FixTray and Shop may be referred to individually as a Party and collectively as the Parties.

## 1. Purpose
FixTray operates a software platform that connects customers and service providers and provides work-order, messaging, scheduling, and payment-related tooling. Shop wishes to use the platform subject to this Agreement.

## 2. Definitions
2.1 Platform means the FixTray website, mobile applications, APIs, messaging tools, and related software services.

2.2 Work Order means a customer job request, estimate, authorization, or service record created in the Platform.

2.3 Service Fee means the per-paid-work-order platform fee charged by FixTray as set forth in the Fee Schedule, Dashboard, or written notice.

2.4 First Contact Event means the earlier of (a) first customer message received by Shop through the Platform, or (b) assignment/creation of a new customer Work Order requiring Shop response.

2.5 Covered Shop Categories means the seven primary shop categories currently supported by FixTray registration and operations:

1. Diesel / Heavy-Duty Services (`diesel`)
2. Gas / Automotive Services (`gas`)
3. Small Engine Services (`small-engine`)
4. Heavy Equipment Services (`heavy-equipment`)
5. Resurfacing / Machining (`resurfacing`)
6. Welding & Fabrication (`welding`)
7. Tire Shop Services (`tire`)

2.6 Mixed / Multi-Category Shop means a Shop that selects `mixed` and provides services in two or more Covered Shop Categories. For contractual purposes, Mixed / Multi-Category Shops are fully subject to this Agreement for all services and Work Orders across all applicable categories.

## 3. Term and Termination
3.1 Term. This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until terminated.

3.2 Termination for Convenience. Either Party may terminate with 30 days written notice.

3.3 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate immediately for material breach if not cured within 10 days after written notice, except where immediate termination is reasonably required for fraud, illegal conduct, or safety/security risk.

3.4 Survival. Sections concerning fees owed, records/audit, limitation of liability, indemnification, confidentiality, dispute resolution, and governing law survive termination.

## 4. Shop Operational Obligations
4.1 Response Time SLA. During Shop Business Hours, Shop must respond to each First Contact Event within 20 minutes. A response must be substantive and customer-facing (not merely an automated acknowledgement).

4.2 Work Order Creation Requirement. Shop must create and maintain a corresponding Work Order in the Platform for each customer job initiated through FixTray or materially facilitated by FixTray communications.

4.3 Data Accuracy. Shop must keep Work Order status, pricing, completion status, and communication logs materially accurate and up to date.

4.4 Service Standards. Shop must perform services in a professional, lawful, and workmanlike manner and maintain all licenses, permits, and insurance required by law, including but not limited to Commercial General Liability (CGL) and Garagekeepers Liability Insurance with limits sufficient to cover the value of customer property in Shop's care.

4.5 Prohibited Conduct. Shop shall not: (a) misrepresent services or pricing, (b) use the Platform for unlawful activities, (c) attempt to evade Service Fees, (d) manipulate reviews or job records, or (e) misuse customer personal information.

## 5. Fees, Billing, and Payment
5.1 Service Fee Obligation. Shop owes the Service Fee for each paid Work Order created through or materially facilitated by FixTray, regardless of payment method.

5.2 Cash and External Processing. If a customer pays in cash, check, ACH, or through Shop's own card processor or any non-FixTray processor, Shop still owes the Service Fee on that Work Order.

5.3 Fee Trigger. Unless otherwise stated in the Fee Schedule, the Service Fee is earned and due when a Work Order is marked paid in full or in final settlement.

5.4 Parts Deposits and Advance Payments. For advance deposits used to procure parts, schedule labor, or reserve capacity before final completion, Service Fee calculation and payment are due upon final invoice settlement or Work Order completion (whichever occurs first), and not on the initial advance deposit alone.

5.5 Reporting and Reconciliation. Shop must accurately record payment method and paid amount for all relevant Work Orders. FixTray may reconcile fees based on Platform records and supporting documentation.

5.6 Invoicing and Payment Terms. FixTray may invoice weekly, biweekly, or monthly. Payment is due within 7 calendar days of invoice date unless otherwise stated in writing.

5.7 Late Charges. Unpaid amounts may accrue interest at 1.5% per month (or the maximum lawful rate, if lower), plus reasonable collection costs.

5.8 Taxes. Shop is responsible for its own taxes arising from services or receipts, excluding taxes based on FixTray net income.

5.9 Pre-Existing Customer Exclusion. A Work Order shall not be deemed materially facilitated by FixTray if Shop can demonstrate, via historical invoicing records, an active business relationship with that specific customer or vehicle VIN within the twelve (12) months prior to the First Contact Event.

## 6. Records and Audit Rights
6.1 Recordkeeping. Shop shall keep complete and accurate books and records for Work Orders and payments for at least 24 months.

6.2 Audit Rights. Upon reasonable notice, FixTray may audit relevant records to verify Work Orders, payment methods, and Service Fees due.

6.3 Underpayment. If an audit identifies underpayment, Shop must promptly pay the deficiency and applicable late charges. If underpayment exceeds 5% for any audited period, Shop will reimburse reasonable audit costs.

## 7. Platform Access and Enforcement
7.1 Access Controls. FixTray may suspend or limit account access for SLA breaches, nonpayment, fraud risk, repeated policy violations, or legal/compliance concerns.

7.2 Corrective Actions. FixTray may require corrective action plans, additional training, or stricter operational thresholds for repeated noncompliance.

7.3 No Circumvention. Shop shall not route jobs off-platform to avoid fee obligations for opportunities sourced through FixTray.

## 8. Customer Communications and Privacy
8.1 Communications. Shop must communicate professionally, timely, and in good faith with customers through approved channels.

8.2 Data Protection. Shop must protect customer information and use it only to perform services and fulfill Work Orders, consistent with applicable privacy laws.

8.3 Security Incidents. Shop must notify FixTray without undue delay of any suspected unauthorized access, data breach, or privacy incident involving Platform-derived customer data.

## 9. Independent Contractor; No Employment
Shop is an independent contractor and is not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of FixTray. Shop is solely responsible for its personnel, wages, taxes, and benefits.

10.2 Limited License. FixTray grants Shop a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable right to use the Platform during the Term, solely for permitted business purposes.

## 18. Service Level Exhibit (Operational)
18.1 SLA Metric: First response to First Contact Event within 20 minutes during Shop Business Hours.

18.1(a) Substantive Response Standard: A substantive response must be manually sent by Shop personnel and must either (i) provide an initial technical triage, estimate path, or next diagnostic step, or (ii) acknowledge receipt and provide a committed diagnostic review or callback window. Purely automated acknowledgements do not satisfy the SLA.

18.2 Measurement Source: Platform timestamps for message receipt, Work Order event, and first human response.

18.3 Exclusions: Force majeure events, Platform-wide outages, and customer messages received outside posted Shop Business Hours (response due within 20 minutes of next Business Hours opening).

18.4 Remedies for Breach: warning, temporary reduced ranking, temporary lead routing limits, suspension, or termination for repeated breach.
